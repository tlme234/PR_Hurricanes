Tracks of Major Hurricanes Relative to Protected & Urban Areas in Puerto Rico (2000-2022)

Authored by: Tiarra Mercado

This map shows major hurricane paths in Puerto Rico from 2000-2022 and vulnerable urban and protected natural areas. I selected this location because I used to live there in my childhood. I sourced various shapefiles through ArcGIS hub and from the USDA Forest Service. I then structured the shapefile layers in a way that was coherent and easily presented. The hurricane track layer was fun to work with and I emphasized the strength of the hurricanes using the symbology features in QGIS. This consisted of modifying both the color and the line width to distinguish the differences in hurricane wind speed. I attempted to use complementary colors which took a lot of trial and error. Once the map graphic was finished I entered into the layout where I started arranging the map frame and the various elements to utilize the full canvas. After more trial and error I added background mask to the text to make it more legible, and separated the legend into two categories.
